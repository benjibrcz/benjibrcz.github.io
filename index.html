About

Currently a research scholar at MATS, investigating goals and beliefs in LLMs.

I'm a machine learning scientist with a background in theoretical physics, currently focused on AI safety research. I completed my PhD at the University of Nottingham, where I designed numerical simulations of quantum matter in curved spacetimes and published in leading physics journals. After transitioning into industry as an ML scientist at Depop, I built large-scale recommender systems using PyTorch, Spark, and cloud infrastructure. In parallel, I pursued AI safety research through AI Safety Camp and independent collaborations, working on mechanistic interpretability and activation steering. I'm now a research scholar at MATS, working on interpreting and evaluating goals and beliefs in large language models.
